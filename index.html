Greenland's Ice Sheets

The Largest in the Northern Hemisphere
  • Ice sheets are giant bodies of glacial ice that were formed as a result of many years of an accumulation of compressed and slightly melted snow that eventually compounded to form massive building blocks of frozen permafrost.
  • There are only two ice sheets left on earth today; they cover most of Antarctica and Greenland.
  • Even though Greenland is the second largest body of ice in the world, the overall size of Greenland's ice sheet is much smaller in comparison to Antartica's continental ice sheets.
  • Almost 80% of Greenland's land surface is covered by a vast tundra of icy glaciers.
  • Ice sheets are important because they hold significant amounts of a reservoir of frozen freshwater.
  • The Antarctic & Greenland ice sheets combine contain more than 99% of the freshwater on Earth.
  • It is estimated that if the whole Greenland ice sheet melted, the global sea level would rise 7 metres.
